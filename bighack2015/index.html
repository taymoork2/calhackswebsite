What is Big Hack?

Presented by Cal Hacks & TreeHacks

Big Hack is an iconic annual 24 hour hackathon organized by the Cal Hacks and TreeHacks teams where 300 students from UC Berkeley and Stanford University come together to build amazing projects.

The day begins on April 4th at 12:00pm with student bus arrivals, lunch, and company API presentations followed by 24 hours of hacking

The event completes with presentations, judging, and prizes

FAQ

We have the Questions and the Answers

WHAT IS A HACKATHON

Hackathons are where your crazy idea becomes reality. Build anything—there are no restrictions on what you can create. Dive in and make the most incredible thing you can alongside fellow creators

WHAT IF I DON'T HAVE A TEAM OR AN IDEA

No problem! Many hackers do not have a fully-formed team or hack concept before the event begins. If you’re a first-time hacker, there will be many others looking for team members with whom you can pair.

HOW BIG SHOULD TEAMS BE

We highly encourage you to build with a team. Teamwork is deeply rooted in the hacking ethos, and you'll have a better time building with your brightest peers. Teams should be at most five people.



WHAT CAN I BUILD

Web, mobile, hardware, you name it. Any type of project is welcome. Projects will be judged based on creativity, technical difficulty, polish, and utility by a panel of industry judges.


WHAT SHOULD
I BRING

All attendees are required to bring their valid student ID for admission. Laptops, sleeping bags, and toiletries are highly recommended.
